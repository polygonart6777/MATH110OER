Solution 1.3.4.1.
According to Hint 1.3.4.1, the formula for the distance between \(\ell\) and \(P\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{dist} (\ell, P) = \norm{(\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{a}) - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}(\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{a})}, \end{equation*}
where \(\mbf{a}\) is a point on the line and \(\mathbf{d}\) is a direction vector of the line. We now only have to plug in the values for the two situations:
  1. For the given \(\ell\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1\\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \mbf{d} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix}, \text{ and } \mbf{p} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} , \end{equation*}
    so that
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{v} := \mathbf{p}-\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\\ -4 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
    We compute
    \begin{align*} \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{d} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \bigl(1\cdot 3 + 1 \cdot (-1) + (-4)\cdot (-2)\bigr) =10. \end{align*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{d} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = 3^2 + (-1)^2 + (-2)^2 = 14, \end{equation*}
    so that (using Hint 1.2.8.1)
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{v} = \left( \frac{\mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{v}}{\mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{d}} \right) \mbf{d} = \tfrac{10}{14}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
    Therefore,
    \begin{align*} \operatorname{dist} (\ell, P) \amp = \norm{\mathbf{v} - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{v}} = \norm{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\\ -4 \end{bmatrix} - \tfrac{10}{14} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix}}\\ \amp = \norm{\tfrac{1}{14}\begin{bmatrix} -16 \\ 24 \\ -36 \end{bmatrix}}\\ \amp = \sqrt{ \left(\tfrac{-16}{14}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{24}{14}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{-36}{14}\right)^2}\\ \amp = \sqrt{\tfrac{152}{14}}. \end{align*}
    Next, for the point \(Q\) on \(\ell\) which has distance \(\sqrt{\tfrac{152}{14}}\) from \(P\text{,}\) we use Hint 1.3.4.2: we need
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{q} := \mbf{a} + \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + \tfrac{10}{14}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1\\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \tfrac{1}{14}\begin{bmatrix} 58 \\ 4\\ 22 \end{bmatrix} , \end{equation*}
    so that \(Q= (\tfrac{29}{7}, \tfrac{2}{7}, \tfrac{11}{7})\text{.}\)
  2. \(P=(1,-1,3)\text{,}\) \(\ell \colon \mbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} +t\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) For the given \(\ell\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\\ -1 \end{bmatrix}, \mbf{d} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix}, \text{ and } \mbf{p} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} , \end{equation*}
    so that
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{v} := \mathbf{p}-\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
    We compute
    \begin{align*} \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{d} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \bigl(0\cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 1 + 4\cdot 4\bigr) =15. \end{align*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{d} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = 3^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 = 26, \end{equation*}
    so that (using Hint 1.2.8.1)
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{v} = \left( \frac{\mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{v}}{\mbf{d}\cdot \mbf{d}} \right) \mbf{d} = \tfrac{15}{26}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
    Therefore,
    \begin{align*} \operatorname{dist} (\ell, P) \amp = \norm{\mathbf{v} - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{v}} = \norm{ \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} - \tfrac{15}{26}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} }\\ \amp = \norm{\tfrac{1}{26}\begin{bmatrix} -45 \\ -41 \\ 44 \end{bmatrix}}\\ \amp = \sqrt{ \left(\tfrac{-45}{26}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{-41}{26}\right)^2 + \left(\tfrac{44}{26}\right)^2}\\ \amp = \sqrt{\tfrac{217}{26}}. \end{align*}
    Next, for the point \(Q\) on \(\ell\) which has distance \(\sqrt{\tfrac{217}{26}}\) from \(P\text{,}\) we use Hint 1.3.4.2: we need
    \begin{equation*} \mbf{q} := \mbf{a} + \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}\mbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} + \tfrac{15}{26}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \tfrac{1}{26}\begin{bmatrix} 71 \\ 15\\ 34 \end{bmatrix} , \end{equation*}
    so that \(Q= (\tfrac{71}{26}, \tfrac{15}{26}, \tfrac{34}{26})\text{.}\)
