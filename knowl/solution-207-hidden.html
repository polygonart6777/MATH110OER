We choose the third row because this choice gives us the most amount of zeros, which means we have less cofactors to compute. For a \(4\times 4 \) matrix,\(A \text{,}\) expanding along the \(3\)rd row,
\begin{equation*} \det(A) = a_{31}cof(A)_{31}+a_{32}cof(A)_{32}+a_{33}cof(A)_{33}+a_{34}cof(A)_{34} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \det(A) = (0)cof(A)_{31}+(0)cof(A)_{32}+(0)cof(A)_{33}+(2)cof(A)_{34} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{34} = (-1)^{3+4}\begin{vmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 2 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 1 \amp 3 \end{vmatrix} \end{equation*}
Let \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 2 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 1 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\) To find the determinant of \(B \) we will expand along the first row, since it has the most amount of zeros.
\begin{equation*} det(B) = b_{11}cof(B)_{11} + b_{12}cof(B)_{12} + b_{13}cof(B)_{13} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} det(B) = (1)cof(B)_{11} + (0)cof(B)_{12} + (0)cof(B)_{13} = (1)(-1)^{1+1}\begin{vmatrix}1 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 3 \end{vmatrix} = 2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{34} = -2 \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} \det(A) \amp = (0)cof(A)_{31}+(0)cof(A)_{32}+(0)cof(A)_{33}+(2)cof(A)_{34} \\ \amp = (2)(-2) \\ \amp = -4 \end{align*}
The determinant of the matrix is -4.
