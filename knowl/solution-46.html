Solution 1.3.7.1.
A normal vector to \(\mathcal{P}_1\) resp. \(\mathcal{P}_2\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \mbf{n}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \text{ resp. } \mbf{n}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
According to the Cosine FormulaÂ 1.2.5.1, the angle between \(\mbf{n}_1\) and \(\mbf{n}_2\) is the number \(\theta\) that satisfies
\begin{equation*} \cos (\theta) = \frac{ \mbf{n}_1\cdot \mbf{n}_2}{ \norm{\mbf{n}_1}\cdot \norm{\mbf{n}_2}} , \end{equation*}
so we compute:
\begin{align*} \mbf{n}_1\cdot \mbf{n}_2 \amp = 1\cdot 2 + 1\cdot 1 + 0\cdot (-1) = 3\\ \norm{\mbf{n}_1} \amp = \sqrt{ 1^2 + 1^2 + 0^2} = \sqrt{2}\\ \norm{\mbf{n}_1} \amp = \sqrt{ 2^2 + 1^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{6}, \end{align*}
so that
\begin{equation*} \cos (\theta) = \frac{ 3}{ \sqrt{2}\cdot \sqrt{6}} = \frac{ 3}{\sqrt{12}} , \text{ i.e. } \sphericalangle (\mathcal{P}_1, \mathcal{P}_2) = \theta = \tfrac{\pi}{6} . \end{equation*}
