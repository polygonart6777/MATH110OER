True: Let us write \(A= [ a_{ij}]\) and \(B= [ b_{jk}]\text{.}\) By assumption, there exists a zero column in \(B\text{,}\) say column \(k_{0}\text{.}\) In other words, \(b_{jk_{0}}=0\) for all \(j\text{.}\) By definition of \(AB= [c_{ik}]\) (see HintÂ 3.1.2.1), its \((i,k)\)-entry is defined by
\begin{equation*} c_{ik} = a_{i1}b_{1k}+a_{i2}b_{2k}+...+a_{in}b_{nk}. \end{equation*}
Since \(b_{jk_{0}}=0\) for all \(j\text{,}\) we see that \(c_{ik_{0}}=0\) for all \(i\text{.}\) In other words, column \(k_{0}\) in \(AB\) is zero.
