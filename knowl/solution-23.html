Solution 2.2.27.1.

By Theorem 2.2.26 there is only one way to do this, namely:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} = \proj_{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\left(\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\end{bmatrix}\right) + \operatorname{perp}_{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\left(\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now it's just a matter of calculating those two vectors.

\begin{align*} \proj_{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\left(\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}\right) \amp = \left(\frac{\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\cdot\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\right)\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \frac{4}{2}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} \\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\end{bmatrix} \end{align*}

And

\begin{align*} \operatorname{perp}_{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\left(\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}\right) \amp = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \proj_{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}\left(\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}\right)\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}-1\\1\\-1\end{bmatrix} \end{align*}

Here is the desired expression:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\1\\-1\end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

You could verify directly that \(\begin{bmatrix}-1\\1\\-1\end{bmatrix} \perp \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) but we don't actually need to, because in Theorem 2.2.26 we proved that the method we used here always works.
