Solution 1.2.5.8.1.
Since
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{w}) = \left( \frac{\mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{w}}{\mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{v}} \right) \mbf{v} , \end{equation*}
we should first compute the dot products:
\begin{align*} \mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{w} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = 1\cdot 1 + 2\cdot 0 + 3\cdot (-2) = -5,\\ \mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{v} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = 1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 = 14, \text{ and so }\\ \amp \frac{\mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{w}}{\mbf{v}\cdot \mbf{v}} = \frac{-5}{14} . \end{align*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{w}) = \frac{-5}{14} \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{-5}{14} \\ \frac{-10}{14} \\ \frac{-15}{14} \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation*}
