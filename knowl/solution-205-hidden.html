For a \(3\times3 \) matrix, expanding along the \(1\)st row,
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{11} = (-1)^{1+1}\begin{vmatrix} 1 \amp 3 \\ 1 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix} =-2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{12} = (-1)^{1+2}\begin{vmatrix} 2 \amp 3 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix}=(-1)(-4)=4 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{13} = (-1)^{1+3}\begin{vmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix}=(-1)(0)=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \det(A) = a_{11}cof(A)_{11}+a_{12}cof(A)_{12}+a_{13}cof(A)_{13}=(1)(-2) + (2)(4)+ (1)(0)=6 \end{equation*}
For a \(3\times3 \) matrix, expanding along the \(2\)nd column,
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{12} = (-1)^{1+2}\begin{vmatrix} 2 \amp 3 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix} =(-1)(-4)=4 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{22} = (-1)^{2+2}\begin{vmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix}=(-1)=-1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} cof(A)_{32} = (-1)^{3+2}\begin{vmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 3 \end{vmatrix}=(-1)(1)=-1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \det(A) = a_{12}cof(A)_{12}+a_{22}cof(A)_{22}+a_{32}cof(A)_{32}=(2)(4) + (1)(-1)+ (1)(-1)=6 \end{equation*}
The determinant of the matrix is 6.
