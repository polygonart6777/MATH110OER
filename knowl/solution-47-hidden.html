A normal vector to the plane is given by
\begin{equation*} \mbf{n} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 7\\ 4 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
The angle \(\theta\) between this vector and the directional vector \(\mbf{d} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) of the line can be computed using the Cosine FormulaÂ 1.2.5.1:
\begin{equation*} \cos (\theta) = \frac{ \mbf{n} \cdot \mbf{d}}{ \norm{\mbf{n}}\cdot \norm{\mbf{d}}} , \end{equation*}
so we compute
\begin{align*} \mbf{n}\cdot \mbf{d} \amp = 4\cdot 1 + 7\cdot 1 + 4\cdot 4 = 27\\ \norm{\mbf{n}} \amp = \sqrt{ 4^2 + 7^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{81} = 9\\ \norm{\mbf{d}} \amp = \sqrt{ 1^2 + 1^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2}. \end{align*}
It follows that
\begin{equation*} \cos (\theta) = \frac{ 27}{ 9\cdot 3\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} , \text{ i.e. } \theta=\tfrac{\pi}{4}. \end{equation*}
The angle \(\alpha\) between the line and the plane is then given by \(\alpha = \tfrac{\pi}{2} - \theta = \tfrac{\pi}{4}.\)
