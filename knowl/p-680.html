(Source: [3], Chapter 11, Exercise 11.2.6)

Suppose \(B = \{\mathbf{u_1},\mathbf{u_2}, \mathbf{u_3}\} \) is an orthogonal basis of \(\mathbb{R}^3 \text{.}\) We have been told that
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{u_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \end{equation*}
but it is not know what \(\mathbf{u_2} \) and \(\mathbf{u_3} \) are. Find the first coordinate of the vector
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
with respect to the basis \(B \text{.}\)
