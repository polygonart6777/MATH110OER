Solution 3.4.6.c.1.
As we have argued in SolutionÂ 3.4.6.b.1, \(\rank (A) = \dim\row(A)\text{,}\) and this number also coincides with \(\dim\col(A)\text{.}\) Thus, \(\dim\col(A) = m\) by assumption. This means, in particular, that among the \(n\) many columns of \(A\text{,}\) there must exist \(m\) many linearly independent ones. Thus, \(m \le n \text{.}\)
