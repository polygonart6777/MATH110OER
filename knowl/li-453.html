Item 3.4.2.c.
Closed under addition: Assume that \(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}\in V\cap W\text{.}\) In particular, \(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y} \in V\) and \(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y} \in W\text{.}\) Since \(V\) and \(W\) are closed under addition, we know that
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y} \in V \quad\text{ and }\quad \mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y} \in W. \end{equation*}
Thus, \(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y} \in V \cap W\text{.}\) This shows that \(V\cap W\) is closed under addition.
