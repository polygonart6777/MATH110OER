Item 3.4.2.b.
Closed under scalar multiplication: Suppose that \(\mathbf{u}\in V\cap W\) and \(\lambda\) is any scalar. By definition of \(V\cap W\text{,}\) we know that \(\mathbf{u} \in V\) and \(\mathbf{u} \in W\text{.}\) Since \(V\) and \(W\) are closed under scalar multiplication, we know that
\begin{equation*} \lambda \cdot \mathbf{u} \in V \quad\text{ and }\quad \lambda \cdot \mathbf{u} \in W. \end{equation*}
Thus, \(\lambda \cdot \mathbf{u} \in V \cap W\text{.}\) This shows that \(V\cap W\) is closed under scalar multiplication.
