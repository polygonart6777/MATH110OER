We will prove (2), leaving the others as exercises. Suppose that \(\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ \vdots \\ v_n\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(\vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix}w_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{z} = \begin{bmatrix}z_1\\ \vdots \\ z_n\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then:

\begin{align*} \vec{v} \cdot (\vec{w} + \vec{z}) \amp = \begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix} \cdot \left(\begin{bmatrix}w_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_n\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}z_1 \\ \vdots \\ z_n\end{bmatrix}\right) \\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix}w_1+z_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_n + z_n \end{bmatrix} \\ \amp = v_1(w_1+z_1) + \cdots + v_n(w_n+z_n) \\ \amp = (v_1w_1 + v_1z+1) + \cdots + (v_nw_n + v_nz_n) \\ \amp = (v_1w_1 + \cdots + v_nw_n) + (v_1z_1 + \cdots + v_nz_n) \\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ \vdots \\ v_n\end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix}w_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}v_1\\ \vdots \\ v_n\end{bmatrix}\cdot \begin{bmatrix}z_1 \\ \vdots \\ z_n\end{bmatrix} \\ \amp = \vec{v} \cdot \vec{w} + \vec{v} \cdot \vec{z} \end{align*}
