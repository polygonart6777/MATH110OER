Solution 1.
By assumption, \(C:= A + B \) is invertible. By the first part of the exercise, we know that \(A^{-1} + B^{-1} = A^{-1}(A + B) B^{-1},\) i.e. \(A^{-1} + B^{-1}= A^{-1}CB^{-1}.\) The matrix \(A^{-1}CB^{-1}\) is invertible as product of invertible matrices by Exercise 3.3.5, which shows that \(A^{-1} + B^{-1}\) is invertible. Exercise 3.3.5 also gives a formula for the inverse of a product, which we use for the equalities \((*)\) in the following computation:
\begin{align*} (A^{-1}CB^{-1})^{-1} \amp=(A^{-1}[CB^{-1}])^{-1} \overset{(*)}{=}[CB^{-1}]^{-1} (A^{-1})^{-1}\\ \amp\overset{(*)}{=}(B^{-1})^{-1} C^{-1} A =B C^{-1} A. \end{align*}
We conclude: The inverse of \(A^{-1} + B^{-1} \) is given by \(B C^{-1} A = B (A + B)^{-1} A.\)
