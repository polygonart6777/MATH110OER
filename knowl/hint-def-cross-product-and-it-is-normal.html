Hint 1.
To find a vector in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) that is normal to a plane, you need to find two non-parallel vectors \(\mbf{a}\) and \(\mbf{b}\) in the plane and compute their cross product
\begin{equation*} \mbf{a}\times \mbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} a_2b_3-a_3b_2\\ a_3b_1-a_1b_3\\ a_1b_2-a_2b_1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
where \(a_i, b_i\) are the three components of \(\mbf{a}\) resp. \(\mbf{b}\text{.}\) This vector is orthogonal to both \(\mbf{a}\) and \(\mbf{b}\) and hence normal to the plane.
